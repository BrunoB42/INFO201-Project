Is there a correlation between health expenditure and healthy life expectancy?

The health expenditure dataset includes a value that is the the percent of Gross Domestic Product(GDP), a representation of the monetary value of goods and services, that each country is spending on health care costs within a year. The question attempts to answer if the % of GDP spent contributes to higher or lower life expectancies. I took the average percent of GDP spent on healthcare and compared it to the average life expectancies for each country.

It appears that there is a slight relationship between average % of GDP spent on health care and life expectancy with a correlation of 0.3122886. The average health expenditure of countries is 6.3464281 % of GDP and the average life expectancy of countries is 62.7465782 years. Since the correlation is weak, this reveals that spending a high percentage of GDP on health care doesn’t necessarily result in higher life expectancy in a country as countries that have spent less % of GDP on healthcare have shown to have high average life expectancy.
